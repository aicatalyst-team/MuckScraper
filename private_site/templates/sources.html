Where The Paper Starts

MuckScraper builds each edition from public RSS feeds, news APIs, and article pages from the open web. The goal is to find important stories, gather coverage from multiple perspectives when available, and generate clear, neutral headlines and summaries.

Additional Articles Found Through News APIs

RSS feeds are only the starting point. Once MuckScraper detects a story, it may look for related coverage through news APIs and public article search results. This helps find additional outlets that may not be part of the standing RSS list, especially when a story is developing quickly or when coverage is concentrated in only one part of the media ecosystem.

That means an article can appear in a MuckScraper story even if the outlet is not listed above as a regular RSS source.

Bias and Balance

MuckScraper attempts to include coverage from across the media spectrum when it is available. Some stories naturally receive coverage from many types of outlets. Others are covered mostly by one side, one region, or one type of publication.

The site does not force artificial balance into every story. Instead, it tries to find important stories from a broad source mix, include different perspectives when available, and avoid letting one outlet's framing dominate the headline or summary.

If you notice a missing source, a bad article grouping, a headline that feels off, or an outlet that should be considered for regular monitoring, email editor-in-chief@muckscraper.news.
